Friday: Conflict

April 18, 2015

It was my first year as a field engineer and I was working on a project on Long Island in New York with one of my general contractors. Throughout the course of the project, the GC would inadverently trip an outdoor sprinkler system causing a small grass area to flood. This did not sit well with the towns chief engineer. As he and I started to discuss the issue, he lost his cool and so did I.

The source of the conflict stemmed from a feeling of being disrespected and disregarded.

Upon analyzing the conflict, I felt annoyed, upset, disrespected and disregarded.

We both lost our cool for about 10 minutes or so and then I asked that we take a step back, cool off and try to resolve the issue. I empathized with him and assured him that my team would put measures in place to avoid tripping the sprinkler system.

If I could go back, I probably would not have lost my cool.

I learned that being accountable for a team can put you in a situation where you have to take responsibility for someone else's actions. I also learned to remain professional at all times.
